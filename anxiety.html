anxiety disorders

Anxiety is a mental health disorder characterized by feelings of worry, anxiety, or fear that are strong enough to interfere with one's daily activities. It is extremely common with more than 3 million US cases per year. Symptoms include stress that's out of proportion to the impact of the event, inability to set aside a worry, and restlessness. Treatments include therapy and self-care. Self-care can entail relaxation techniques, physical exercise, and stress management.

additional symptoms include:

  • rapid breathing
  • increased heart rate
  • having a sense of impending doom, danger, or panic
  • upset stomach
  • sweating, chills, or hot flashes
